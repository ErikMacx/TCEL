**Everyone is buying AI. Almost no one can prove it is working.**

The most useful AI question this fortnight is not which tool to buy. It is whether the tools you already pay for have moved a single number you report to your board.

Across the market the pattern is the same: almost every company is buying AI, and almost none can show it in their margins. That gap has a name, the productivity paradox, and for the CEO of a group it is the whole game.

There are only two honest readings of it. Either AI is working and your measurement is too crude to see it, or it is not working and the spend is theatre. Both are expensive, in different ways. The first means value is leaking out unrecorded, so you cannot double down on what is paying off. The second means budget is going to *look* modern rather than to *be* better.

The way through is not more tools. It is to pick one process that actually matters, baseline it honestly, apply AI to that one thing, and measure the same number ninety days later. If it does not move, stop. AI belongs inside a strategy you are already executing, not bolted on top of it.

**One question to chew on this fortnight:**
If your CFO asked tomorrow which line of the P&L your AI spend has moved, could anyone answer with a number?

That is it for this fortnight. Something to sit with, not a to-do list. Reply if it lands, or if it does not.
