A CEO owes the owning family two things above all: candour, and a clear line. The family owns the group and sets the appetite for risk and return. The CEO runs the business to that appetite and reports honestly on it.

Most friction in family businesses is not a bad relationship. It is a blurred line, owners drawn into operations, or a CEO setting an appetite that was never theirs to set.
