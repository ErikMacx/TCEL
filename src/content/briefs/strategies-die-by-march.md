A strategy dies in March for a simple reason: nothing in the operating year is built to hold it. The plan is signed off in January, the binder goes on a shelf, and by the end of the first quarter every business is back to running on last year's habits.

The work is not to write a better document. It is to turn the strategy into a discipline: a short set of objectives every business owns, a small number of measures that matter, and a quarterly rhythm where progress is reviewed and choices are re-made out loud.
