Resist the urge to act on day one. The first ninety days are for listening: to the people who run the work, the numbers, and the things said only in the corridor.

Your job in that window is to find the one or two truths everyone already knows but no one will say, and to earn the right to act on them. Change made before that right is earned rarely holds.
